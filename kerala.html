Kerala....Gods own Country

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, is often proudly referred to as "God's Own Country",Lying in the southwestern tip of India, the green strip of Kerala sandwiched between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea is unique and stands apart from the rest of the states in the country. This sentiment is reflected not only in its lush landscapes, tranquil backwaters, and picturesque beaches but most significantly, in the state's rich tapestry of cultural heritage. The art forms of Kerala, vibrant and diverse, are an eloquent testament to its centuries-old traditions, rendering music, dance, and visual narratives into a unique sensory experience.Let us set out on a voyage through the history and culture of this land that is inimitable.

Kadhakali

Kadhakali is a traditional form of classical Indian dance, and one of the oldest theater forms still practiced. It is a "story game" genre of art, but one distinguished by the elaborately colourful make-up and costumes of the traditional male actor-dancers. It is almost entirely practiced by Malayali people.The roots of Kathakali are unclear.

Ottanthullal

Ottanthullal is a recite-and-dance art-form of Kerala, India. It was introduced in the eighteenth century by Kunchan Nambiar, one of the Prachina Kavithrayam. The folksy performance,often laced with humour intended at criticism of society, is accompanied by a mridangam a pair of ilathalam cymbals.

Koothu:Koothu is a socio-religious art performed in the Koothambalam or the Koothuthara of temples, either independently or as part of Kutiyattam.

kudiyattam: Koodiyattam is a traditional performing art form in the state of Kerala.It is a combination of ancient Sanskrit theatre with elements of Koothu.

These art forms, passed down through generations, are more than aesthetic expressions. They serve as mirrors to the state's historical chronicles, folklores, religious mores and the collective consciousness of its people. Evoking emotions, telling tales, and reflecting philosophies, the Kerala art forms make the distinction between art and life blur, turning them into virtual synonyms. This article provides an in-depth look into some of those mesmerizing art forms and their space within Kerala's rich cultural heritage. From the riveting dance dramas of Kathakali and Mohiniyattam to the captivating rhythms of Panchavadyam and Koodiyattam, the art of Kerala is an aesthete's paradise. Each is a unique story - an unfolding pageant of human creativity and genius, rooted in spirituality and the very fabric of Keralite life. Embark on this journey to glimpse the vibrant palette of Keralite culture - a world where art breathes life, tells stories and keeps an ancient heritage alive.The signature performing arts of Kerala, lovingly christened as "God's Own Country," have a captivating charm that resonates with all spectators. Whether performed on the grand stage or the humble roadside, the real allure of these performances lies not in their setting but in the mesmerizing artists themselves.

Theyyam,Meaning "God" or the "incarnation of God", theyyam is an ancient folk ritual combining theatre, mime and worship that predates Hinduism.
